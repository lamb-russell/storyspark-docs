Story Spark

Voice-first interactive adventures for kids aged 4–7, told out loud on your iPhone.

What is Story Spark?

Story Spark is an iPhone storytelling app. A warm, natural narrator voice tells a short adventure starring a hero your child chooses, and your child steers the story by talking back — no reading or typing required. Each story runs a classic arc (opening, rising action, a twist, a climax, and a cozy wrap-up), and every answer your child gives shapes what happens next.

The narration is generated on your phone. Story Spark uses the open-source Kokoro neural text-to-speech model running locally via kokoro-ios and Apple's MLX framework — far more natural than the built-in iOS voices, and no audio ever leaves the device.

How it works

  1. Pick a hero and a place — or tap the dice for a surprise. You can speak the character's name and role using the mic buttons in Story Setup.
  2. Tap Start Story and listen. The narrator reads each scene aloud.
  3. When the narrator asks what happens next, tap the big button and talk! Your child's answer becomes part of the story.

Favorite heroes are saved automatically, so recurring characters can come back for "another adventure" with one tap when a story ends. Story length (Short, Medium, Long) and story flavor (Sweet, Salty, Zesty, Spicy — from extra-cozy to big silly thrills) are adjustable in the settings sheet.

Choosing a story model

Story Spark can generate stories two ways. Pick in the gear menu under Story model.

Option 1: On-device (Apple) — no account needed

On iPhones that support Apple Intelligence (iPhone 15 Pro or newer, iOS 26+), Story Spark can use Apple's built-in on-device language model. Stories generate entirely on the phone — no API key, no internet, no rate limits.

Enable it in Settings → Apple Intelligence & Siri on your iPhone, then pick On-device (Apple) at the top of the model list in Story Spark. Note: Apple's model has strict built-in content filters. If the narrator says it "cannot tell this one," try a gentler hero or setting — or switch to a Gemini model, which handles imaginative kid-story material more flexibly.

Option 2: Google Gemini — richer stories, needs a free API key

Google's Gemini models write more inventive prose. You'll need a free API key:

  1. Visit aistudio.google.com/apikey and sign in with a Google account.
  2. Click Create API key and copy it.
  3. In Story Spark, open the gear menu, paste the key under Gemini API key, and tap Save to Keychain. The key is stored only in your device's Keychain.
  4. Pick a model under Story model. The list is fetched live from Google, so it always shows what your key can use. gemini-flash-latest is a great default; a flash-lite model gives the most generous free-tier limits.

Google's free tier allows roughly 10–15 requests per minute and ~1,500 per day for Flash-class models. A full story uses about 4–8 requests, so free-tier limits are effectively invisible in normal family use. Live quotas for your key are shown at aistudio.google.com/rate-limit.

Tips & troubleshooting

The narrator's voice doesn't play, or is very quiet

Check the silent switch and volume first. Narration plays through the loudspeaker; if you're on Bluetooth headphones or a speaker, it follows those. The speaker icon in the top-left corner shows green when the Kokoro voice is ready.

Tap any story line to hear it again

Every narrator bubble has a small speaker icon — tap the bubble to replay that line.

"The storyteller cannot tell this one"

Apple's on-device model declined the scene due to its built-in safety filters, which can be overly cautious with spooky or action-y kid themes. Adjust the hero or setting, or switch to a Gemini model in the gear menu.

"Model not found" errors with Gemini

Google retires model names over time. Open the gear menu and tap Refresh model list, then pick a current model. Rolling aliases like gemini-flash-latest avoid this problem.

Speech recognition misses words

Recognition happens on-device via iOS. It transcribes as your child speaks — the text appears under the talk button, and the fields stay editable if a name comes out wrong. "Type instead" under the talk button is always available as a fallback.
